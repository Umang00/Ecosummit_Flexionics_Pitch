## YouTube Analysis — What Real EcoSummit Pitchers Actually Do

*4 YouTube pitch videos added to NotebookLM and analysed (2026-03-18). Full transcripts queried.*

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Key finding: The investment ask goes at the END — not Slide 1.** EcoSummit recommends it on slide 1, but **ALL 4 actual pitches kept the ask for the final slide.** We should follow this pattern. Investors don't decide in the first 20 seconds — they decide after you've built the story.

| Pitch | First Words / Opening | Ask Placement | Ask Amount | Climate Change? |
|-------|----------------------|---------------|------------|----------------|
| **Daniel Wiegand – Lilium Aviation (ECO15)** | "Imagine a world in which you could fly every day everywhere with the freedom of a bird…" | **Last slide** | €500k | No — said "zero CO2" but not "climate change" |
| **Christoph Sollich – Pitch Doctor (ECO16)** | Story-driven: "My startup is called Berlin and we're in the startup home as a service business…" | **Not stated** | No specific amount | No |
| **Dainis Kruze – Aerones** | Mission: "We build robots for wind turbine blade maintenance and inspections" | **Last slide** | "Going for Series A" (vague) | Yes — explicitly said "to fight the climate change" |
| **Nils Aldag – Sunfire** | Value prop: "We develop and produce industrial electrolyzers to bring renewable energy to hard to abate sectors" | **Last slide** | €10–15M Series D | No — used "Fit for 55" regulatory frame instead |

### Common successful patterns:
1. **Open with a vivid hook or clear company mission** — not climate problem framing
2. **Lead with a clear, memorable business concept** you can say in one sentence
3. **Quantify impact with specific numbers** — the bigger and more specific the better
4. **Show traction / proof of work** — "here is what we've done, not what we plan to do"
5. **State the ask clearly at the END** — after you've built conviction (not at the start)
6. **Don't mention climate change** — Lilium, Sunfire, Pitch Doctor all avoided it; investors know the context
